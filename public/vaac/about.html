About the Project

Understanding the science behind digital eye testing

What is Visual Acuity Analysis & Compensation?

Visual Acuity Analysis & Compensation (VAAC) is a digital screening system that measures how clearly a person can see and how well they perceive colors. The goal is to give users an early indication of possible vision issues so they can seek professional advice in time.

Why Eye Testing is Important

Our eyes are one of the most used organs in modern life. Long screen hours, poor lighting, and aging can silently weaken vision. Regular testing helps detect problems early — preventing strain, headaches and long-term damage.

Contrast Sensitivity Testing

Contrast sensitivity is the ability to distinguish an object from its background, especially when colors are similar. Our test gradually reduces the contrast and size of the symbols, simulating real-world conditions like driving at night or reading faded text.

Color Blindness Testing

Around 1 in 12 men and 1 in 200 women have some form of color vision deficiency. Using Ishihara-style plates with carefully chosen red-green palettes, this test helps identify normal vision, partial color blindness, or possible red-green deficiency.

Benefits of Digital Healthcare Systems

Digital healthcare tools like VAAC make basic screening accessible, affordable, and instant. They reduce hospital visits, store user history, and encourage people to take charge of their own health using technology they already own.
